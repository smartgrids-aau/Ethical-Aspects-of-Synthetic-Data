#	Ethical Aspects of Synthetic Data
AI Ethics  •  Data Privacy  •  GDPR  •  Bias

Synthetic data is generated data, created to statistically resemble real, sensitive data — such as financial records or household power consumption. It is designed as a privacy-friendly alternative for training AI models and modeling applications. While not containing explicit instances of original records, synthetic data reflects the traces, patterns, and distributions found in the source data and therefore still carries information about the people it is based on.  This project explores the legal, ethical, and moral aspects of using synthetic data for modeling and AI training. It focuses on governance and policy challenges, including data privacy laws (GDPR) and the risk of 'laundering' historical and systemic biases through the data generation process.

## Research Questions
*	How do data protection laws (such as GDPR) apply to synthetic data? Is it personal data even though it was generated?
*	Beyond direct re-identification, what inferences can be drawn from synthetic data?
*	Biases present in the original data will be reflected in the synthetic data — what are the implications, and are there solutions?
*	What legal or policy-based solutions could address the ethical risks of synthetic data?
